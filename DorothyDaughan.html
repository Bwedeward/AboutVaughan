Bio

Dorothy Johnson Vaughan (September 20, 1910 â€“ November 10, 2008) was an African American mathematician and human computer who worked for the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA), and NASA, at Langley Research Center in Hampton, Virginia. In 1949, she became acting supervisor of the West Area Computers, the first African-American woman to supervise a staff at the center.

Important Contributions

Dorothy Vaughan was an important contributor to the early U.S. space program.

  • Worked for the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA)
  • Promoted to lead the West Area Computing Unit ("West Computers") in 1949, despite NACA being segregated and black employees having to use separate facilities
    • The West Computers was a group of black female mathematicians considered "human computers" who performed complex computations and analyzed data for aerospace engineers
    • Through this promotion, Vaughan became the first black supervisor and one of the few female supervisors within NACA
    • Vaughan supervised the West Computers until 1958 when NACA was incorporated into the newly created NASA and the segregated facilities were closed
  • Joined the NASA Analysis and Computation Division, a group of men and women of all races, with many other West Computers
  • Became an expert at FORTRAN, a computer programming language used for scientific and algebraic applications, as NASA had begun using electronic computers
